# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist for College Students

## General Online Safety
1. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV with anyone — 
   not even someone claiming to be from your bank.
2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important 
   accounts — email, social media, and banking apps.
3. Use strong, unique passwords for each platform. 
   Use a password manager if needed.
4. Always verify links before clicking — check that the URL 
   starts with https:// and matches the official domain.
5. Do not install apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer if asked 
   by an unknown caller — these give them full access to 
   your phone.
6. Regularly check your bank statements and UPI transaction 
   history for any suspicious activity.

## Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)
7. Remember: To RECEIVE money on UPI, you never need 
   to enter your PIN or approve a request. 
   If someone says otherwise — it is a scam.
8. Never scan a QR code sent by a stranger to "receive" 
   a payment — QR codes on UPI are only for sending money.

## Reporting
If you are a victim of cybercrime:
- 🌐 Report online: **cybercrime.gov.in**
- 📞 Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930** (24x7)
- Report immediately — the faster you report, the higher 
  the chance of recovering funds.
