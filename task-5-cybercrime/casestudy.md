# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

## Type of Cybercrime
UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## What is it?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of cybercrime 
where fraudsters trick victims into sending money or revealing 
sensitive payment credentials through deception, impersonation, 
or fake links.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Contact** — The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, 
   WhatsApp, or SMS, posing as a bank representative, seller, 
   or someone offering a reward/refund.

2. **Hook** — They tell the victim they need to "verify" their 
   account, claim a cashback reward, or receive a refund for 
   a recent transaction.

3. **Deception** — The fraudster sends a fake UPI payment 
   REQUEST (not a payment) and tells the victim to "approve 
   it to receive money." The victim, confused, approves — 
   and money is deducted instead.

4. **Escalation** — In some cases, the fraudster convinces 
   the victim to install a screen-sharing app (like AnyDesk), 
   giving them full access to the victim's phone and UPI app.

5. **Loss** — Money is transferred out within seconds. 
   UPI transactions are instant and often irreversible.

## Who is Usually Targeted?
- First-time smartphone users and students
- Online shoppers on platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace
- People expecting refunds or job offers
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with digital payments

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss (cases range from ₹500 to ₹50 lakhs)
- Victims often feel ashamed and delay reporting
- Recovery of funds is difficult once transferred
- Psychological stress and loss of trust in digital payments
